                           Sales Analysis Report: Savanna Fibre (2025)
Project Overview
This report provides a detailed analysis of the sales performance and customer growth of Savanna Fibre for the 2025 fiscal period. The objective of this study was to identify high-performing revenue streams, evaluate the effectiveness of customer acquisition strategies across different fiber packages, and monitor overall business scaling.

Executive Summary (Key Sales Metrics)
The business demonstrated healthy financial performance with significant recurring revenue from renewals.

Total Gross Revenue: UGX 111,301,000

Average Daily Sales (Revenue): UGX 304,934

Total New Customer Sales: 16,358 units

Total Subscription Renewals: 116,347 units

Final Active Subscriber Base: 409 (Net Growth of +37)

Revenue Analysis by Product Tier
We categorized sales performance across various packages (Twiga, Ndovu, Simba, Chui, etc.) to determine which products contribute most to the bottom line.

Top Revenue Drivers
Twiga_72K (Market Leader): This package is the primary revenue engine, generating UGX 23,409,000. Its price point and speed combination appear to hit the "sweet spot" for the majority of the market.

Ndovu_253K (Acquisition Hook): While mid-tier in price, this package led in volume for new sales, onboarding 3,326 new customers. It serves as the primary entry point for new users.

Pricing & Value Correlation
The analysis shows that while high-speed "Enterprise" and "Corporate" packages (500Mbps+) have higher individual ticket prices, the bulk of the company's revenue stability comes from the high-volume, lower-priced "Home" packages like Twiga and Chui.

Customer Acquisition & Growth Trends
Using a 7-day rolling average to smooth out daily sales volatility, we observed the following:

Growth Velocity: The business achieved a 9.9% increase in the total customer base over the period.

Daily Onboarding: On average, Savanna Fibre signs up approximately 45 new customers per day.

The "Stickiness" Factor: The ratio of renewals to new customers is approximately 7:1, indicating extremely high customer retention and a successful subscription-based business model.

Visual Insights Summary
As part of this analysis, 11 automated visualizations were generated:

Revenue Concentration: Pie charts confirm that a few key packages drive over 40% of the total revenue.

Activity Composition: Stacked area charts highlight that renewals are the primary daily activity, providing consistent cash flow compared to the more volatile new-customer sales.

Performance Heatmap: High correlation was found between Payment_UGX and Speed_Mbps, validating that customers are willing to pay a premium for higher bandwidth.

Strategic Recommendations
Bundle Incentives: Offer a "Ndovu-to-Simba" upgrade discount. Since Ndovu is the top acquisition tool, moving those users to higher-margin tiers like Simba will accelerate revenue growth.

Retention Loyalty: Implement a "6-Month Renewal" bonus. Given the high renewal volume, locking in users for longer periods will reduce the risk of monthly churn.

Marketing Focus: Focus digital ad spend on the Twiga_72K package, as it has the highest proven ROI for the business.

Technical Methodology
To ensure data integrity and reproducibility, the following pipeline was used:

Data Cleaning: Handled in Mysql to ensure date consistency and remove outliers.

Analytics: Automated KPI calculation using custom Mysql and Python scripts.

Reporting: Visualizations created using Matplotlib and Seaborn.

Interactive BI: A Streamlit dashboard was developed for real-time sales monitoring.
